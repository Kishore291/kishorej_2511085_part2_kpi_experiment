## Task 1: Business Problem Statement
* **Decision to be made:** Whether to launch the new onboarding and activation campaign (Treatment) to 100% of the user base or stick with the existing experience (Control).
* **Who the decision impacts:** All new users signing up for the subscription-based digital product.
* **Metric that should improve:** The primary focus is improving the Paid Conversion Rate.
* **Risks to monitor:** We must ensure the new campaign does not result in unintended negative consequences, specifically an increase in Support Ticket Rates or Refund Rates, which would indicate user confusion or dissatisfaction.
* **Evidence required:** A statistically significant improvement in our North Star metric (Paid Conversion Rate) in the Treatment group, without a statistically significant degradation in our guardrail metrics.

## Task 2: North Star Metric Definition
* **Selected North Star Metric:** Paid Conversion Rate (`converted_to_paid` / total users).
* **Why it is the main metric:** The ultimate goal of onboarding is not just to get users to click around, but to demonstrate enough value that they pay for the subscription. This metric directly measures the campaign's success in achieving that financial outcome.
* **Why others are supporting metrics:** Metrics like "Trial Start Rate" and "Onboarding Completion Rate" are vital *leading indicators*, but they are not the North Star because a user completing onboarding does not guarantee they will generate revenue.
* **Connection to business growth:** An increase in Paid Conversion Rate directly increases Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) and customer lifetime value.
* **Risks of blind optimization:** If we optimize solely for Paid Conversion Rate blindly, we might use overly aggressive sales tactics during onboarding. This could trick users into paying, resulting in a massive spike in refunds and support tickets days later, ultimately damaging the brand and net revenue.
